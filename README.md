Abstract: To address spatio-temporal reconstruction redundancy, single-modal ambiguity, dimension fragmentation and error accumulation in the entire lifecycle of automation in construction via digital twin (DT), we are the first to propose a modular, innovative spatio-temporally incremental DT (STIDT) paradigm with replaceable state-of-the-art modules, integrating AI-driven multi-temporal unmanned aerial vehicle nap-of-the-object photogrammetry, bimodal instance (monolithic-semantic) 3D modelling, and unreal engine (UE) for 5D (i.e., 3D space + 1D time + 1D semantics) spatio-temporal model foundation. The key advantage of our STIDT paradigm is spatio-temporally incremental updating: via change detection technology, only changed areas are reconstructed, preserving unchanged areas to boost efficiency. Furthermore, theoretical derivation of spatiotemporal error propagation is given. Experimental results show: mIoU=0.612 for 3D instance segmentation, OA=0.876 for change detection, RMSE=0.417dm, 33% efficiency gain (41h saved), and successful UE-based dynamic simulation in solar illumination, feature shadows and weather time. Our paradigm can provide new methodology for urban intelligent construction.
